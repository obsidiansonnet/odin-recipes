Chhole

Description

Chhole is a classic North Indian chickpea curry known for its rich, spicy, and tangy flavors. Made with boiled chickpeas simmered in a masala of onions, tomatoes, and aromatic spices, it's a staple dish often enjoyed with bhature, rice, or roti. Chhole holds a special place in Indian cuisine, offering both comfort and bold taste in every bite.

Ingredients

  • Dried chickpeas (chana) ─ 1 cup (soaked overnight)
  • Onion ─ 1 large, finely chopped
  • Tomato ─ 2 medium, pureed
  • Ginger-garlic paste ─ 1 tsp
  • Chhole masala ─ 2 tsp
  • Turmeric powder ─ ½ tsp
  • Red chili powder ─ 1 tsp
  • Cumin seeds ─ 1 tsp
  • Oil or ghee ─ 2 tbsp
  • Salt ─ to taste

Steps

  1. Soak & Boil Chickpeas: Soak chickpeas overnight, then pressure cook with water and a pinch of salt until soft (about 4─5 whistles).
  2. Make the Masala: Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, sauté onions until golden brown.
  3. Add Tomatoes & Spices: Stir in ginger-garlic paste, then add tomato puree, turmeric, red chili, and chhole masala. Cook until oil separates.
  4. Combine & Simmer: Add boiled chickpeas to the masala and mix well. Simmer for 10─15 minutes to let flavors blend.
  5. Garnish & Serve: Garnish with chopped coriander leaves. Serve hot with bhature, puri, or jeera rice.
