Gobi Manchurian (Dry)

Description

Dry Gobi Manchurian is a popular Indo-Chinese appetizer made with crispy batter-fried cauliflower florets tossed in a flavorful mix of garlic, chilies, soy sauce, and spices. It's a street food favorite that's spicy, tangy, and perfect as a snack or starter. Unlike the gravy version, the dry style has no sauce base. The fried cauliflower is coated just enough to give a glossy finish, while keeping the florets crisp and crunchy.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium cauliflower (cut into small florets)
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup cornflour
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp red chili sauce
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • Oil for deep frying

Steps

  1. Boil cauliflower florets in salted water for 3─4 minutes, then drain completely.
  2. Make a thick batter using all-purpose flour, cornflour, ginger-garlic paste, salt, and a little water.
  3. Dip florets into the batter and deep fry them until golden brown and crispy. Set aside.
  4. In a separate pan, heat 1─2 tbsp oil and saut√© garlic, green chilies, and onions until slightly browned.
  5. Add soy sauce and chili sauce, stir quickly on high heat.
  6. Add fried cauliflower and toss well to coat; cook for another minute and serve hot.
